üßÆ Percentage

Percentage is a fundamental concept used in almost every quantitative topic. Mastering these formulas is key to solving problems related to Profit & Loss, Simple Interest, and Data Interpretation.

  • üìà Percentage of a Value: $$ \text{Percentage} = \frac{\text{Value}}{\text{Total Value}} \times 100 $$
  • ‚¨ÜÔ∏è Percentage Increase: $$ \text{Increase} \% = \frac{\text{New Value} - \text{Original Value}}{\text{Original Value}} \times 100 $$
  • ‚¨áÔ∏è Percentage Decrease: $$ \text{Decrease} \% = \frac{\text{Original Value} - \text{New Value}}{\text{Original Value}} \times 100 $$

üö∂ Speed, Time, Distance

This topic is crucial for solving problems on trains, boats, and general motion. Pay close attention to unit conversions.

  • üí® Speed = $$ \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} $$
  • ‚è±Ô∏è Time = $$ \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} $$
  • üõ£Ô∏è Distance = $$ \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} $$
  • üîÑ Conversion: km/hr to m/s = $$ \times \frac{5}{18} $$
  • ‚ÜîÔ∏è Average Speed = $$ \frac{\text{Total Distance Travelled}}{\text{Total Time Taken}} $$

üí∞ Simple Interest (SI)

Simple Interest is a straightforward calculation on the principal amount. These formulas are commonly tested in banking and other finance-related exams.

  • üè¶ Simple Interest: $$ \text{SI} = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100} $$
    where P = Principal, R = Rate (in % p.a.), T = Time (in years)
  • üí≤ Total Amount (A) = $$ P + \text{SI} $$

üí± Compound Interest (CI)

Compound Interest is calculated on both the principal and the accumulated interest. Pay attention to the compounding frequency.

  • üìà Amount (A): $$ A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T $$
    where P = Principal, R = Rate (in % p.a.), T = Time (in years)
  • üí∏ Compound Interest (CI): $$ \text{CI} = A - P = P \left[ \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T - 1 \right] $$
  • üóìÔ∏è When compounded half-yearly: $$ A = P \left(1 + \frac{R/2}{100}\right)^{2T} $$
  • üóìÔ∏è When compounded quarterly: $$ A = P \left(1 + \frac{R/4}{100}\right)^{4T} $$

üìê Area & Volume

Memorizing these geometry formulas is a must for Mensuration questions. These cover common 2D and 3D shapes.

  • ‚èπÔ∏è Area of Square: $$ A = \text{side}^2 $$ | Perimeter: $$ P = 4 \times \text{side} $$
  • üì¶ Volume of Cube: $$ V = \text{side}^3 $$ | Surface Area: $$ \text{SA} = 6 \times \text{side}^2 $$
  • ‚ö´ Area of Circle: $$ A = \pi r^2 $$ | Circumference: $$ C = 2\pi r $$
  • üî∫ Area of Triangle: $$ A = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} $$
  • cylindrical_drum Volume of Cylinder: $$ V = \pi r^2 h $$
    r = radius, h = height

üìä Average

The concept of average is simple, but problems often involve variations or weighted averages.

  • ‚öñÔ∏è Average = $$ \frac{\text{Sum of Observations}}{\text{Number of Observations}} $$
  • ‚öñÔ∏è Weighted Average = $$ \frac{w_1 x_1 + w_2 x_2 + ...}{w_1 + w_2 + ...} $$

üî¢ Permutation & Combination

This topic deals with arrangements and selections. Understanding the difference between a permutation (order matters) and a combination (order doesn't matter) is key.

  • üîÄ Permutation (nPr): $$ P(n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!} $$
  • üé≤ Combination (nCr): $$ C(n, r) = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} $$
    n = total items, r = items to choose

üßÆ HCF & LCM

The relationship between HCF, LCM, and the numbers themselves is an important formula to remember for quick calculations.

  • üîó Product of two numbers = $$ \text{HCF} \times \text{LCM} $$
  • ‚ûó HCF of Fractions = $$ \frac{\text{HCF of Numerators}}{\text{LCM of Denominators}} $$
  • ‚úñÔ∏è LCM of Fractions = $$ \frac{\text{LCM of Numerators}}{\text{HCF of Denominators}} $$
