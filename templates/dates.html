A chronological understanding of key dates and milestones is crucial for answering static GK questions and understanding historical context. This comprehensive timeline provides a year-by-year overview of the most significant events that have shaped India's journey, from the establishment of the East India Company to the modern era.

British East India Company was granted a royal charter by Queen Elizabeth I, marking the beginning of British influence in India. This event is a critical starting point for modern Indian history.

British forces, led by Robert Clive, defeated Siraj-ud-Daulah, the Nawab of Bengal. This victory was a turning point that marked the formal beginning of British rule and expansion in India.

Fought between the Marathas and Ahmad Shah Abdali, this battle resulted in a devastating loss for the Marathas, weakening their power and paving the way for British dominance in the subcontinent.

This was the first significant step taken by the British government to regulate and control the affairs of the British East India Company in India, setting a precedent for future governance.

A treaty signed between the British and the Marathas, officially ending the First Anglo-Maratha War. It established a period of peace and stability for two decades, benefiting both sides.

Launched by Mahatma Gandhi on August 8, this mass protest demanded an immediate end to British rule in India. It was a decisive movement that weakened British authority.
