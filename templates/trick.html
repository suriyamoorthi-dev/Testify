üßÆ Percentage

To find 5% of a number quickly: Simply divide the number by 20.

To find 10% of a number: Just move the decimal one place to the left. 10% of 750 is 75.0.

üë∑ Time & Work

Use the **LCM (Least Common Multiple) Method** to find the total work.
Example: A can do a work in 10 days, B in 15 days. LCM of 10 and 15 is 30. Total work = 30 units. A's efficiency = 30/10 = 3 units/day. B's efficiency = 30/15 = 2 units/day. Together, they complete 5 units/day, so they finish in 30/5 = 6 days.

Example: A 10% discount followed by a 20% discount is equivalent to a single discount of: (-10) + (-20) + ((-10) √ó (-20))/100 = -30 + 200/100 = -30 + 2 = -28%. So, a 28% total discount.

üß† Square of Numbers Ending in 5

This is a fantastic mental math trick. If a number ends in 5, its square ends in 25. To find the rest, take the digit before the 5 (let's call it 'x'), and multiply it by the next consecutive integer (x+1). Then, just add 25 at the end.
Example: For 65¬≤, 'x' is 6. So, 6 √ó (6+1) = 6 √ó 7 = 42. Add 25 at the end to get 4225.

üî° Coding-Decoding (Alphabet Positions)

Memorize the alphabetical position of letters with a mnemonic like "EJOTY" (E=5, J=10, O=15, T=20, Y=25). This helps you quickly calculate the position of any letter (e.g., K is J+1, so 11; S is T-1, so 19).

üìÜ Calendar Trick (Odd Day Method)

The key is to find the number of "odd days" (the remainder after dividing the total days by 7).
Formula: Total days √∑ 7 = Quotient + Remainder (Odd Days).
Use this to count forward or backward from a known date to find the day of the week.
